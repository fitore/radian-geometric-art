Construction Notes
Begin with a base circle of your chosen radius. Inscribe a square using horizontal and vertical diameters. Rotate a second square 45° by marking compass arcs at the diagonal intersections. The 8-pointed star emerges at the intersection points of both squares. This is a beginner-friendly construction requiring only 6 compass operations and 8 straight lines.

Construction Sequence
Triquetra 6 steps · ~15 min · Beginner ⚠ Experimental
1 Draw the base circle
◎ compass
Set your compass to radius R — this will be the outer boundary of the knot. Place the compass point at center O and draw a complete circle.
→ Produces: Base circle defining the pattern's extent
2 Mark three equidistant points
◎ compass
Without changing the compass width, place the point on the circle's top. Walk the compass around the circle, marking intersections. Every second mark gives you three equidistant points at 120° intervals — the vertices of your inscribed equilateral triangle.
→ Produces: Points A, B, C at 120° intervals on the base circle
3 Draw three overlapping circles
◎ compass
Reduce compass to ⅔R. Place point at A, draw a full circle. Repeat from B and C. These three circles define the curved paths of the triquetra.
→ Produces: Three overlapping circles creating the knot framework
4 Trace the continuous knot path
✎ pen
Starting from the top intersection, trace along the arc of circle A clockwise until you reach the intersection with circle B. Continue along circle B's arc, then onto circle C, returning to your starting point. You should have a continuous closed path.
→ Produces: The outer boundary of the triquetra knot
5 Add the interlace crossings
✎ pen ▬ straightedge
At each of the three crossing points, establish the over-under pattern. The convention is: each arm passes over one crossing and under the next, creating a true knot. Erase the overlapped segments and redraw with the gap to show the weaving.
→ Produces: The characteristic Celtic interlace at three crossing points
6 Ink the final form
✎ pen
Trace the final knot path with your chosen pen weight. Add consistent line thickness — traditional Celtic knotwork uses uniform stroke width. Optionally add a center point where the three arcs converge.
→ Produces: Completed triquetra with clean interlace crossings
