Radian — menu improvements

Two directions · responsive · same palette

What's wrong with today's menu, and what changed

  • Too many chips at once. 8 groups with ~45 chips compete visually with the art. Groups now collapse, each with an active-count badge.
  • No active-state summary. A compact Filters bar sits above the grid so you always see what's applied — one-click remove per pill.
  • Weak hierarchy. Small sets (Status, Difficulty) become segmented controls; symmetry becomes a numeric grid; long lists stay as chips. Kebab-case labels → sentence case.
  • No responsive story. Rail narrows at 1200, collapses at 760 behind a Filters bottom-sheet. Header condenses secondary actions into an overflow.
  • Variation B replaces the rail with a popover toolbar above the grid — returns ~280px of canvas for the art.
