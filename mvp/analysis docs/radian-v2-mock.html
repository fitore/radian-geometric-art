Construction Notes This is a classic 8-pointed star rosette from two overlapping rotated squares inscribed in a circle. Begin with a base circle, inscribe a square, rotate a copy 45°, and extend the intersections. The surrounding kite shapes emerge from connecting adjacent star tips. Intermediate difficulty — straightforward construction but precision matters at star points.

Rotating Squares
Traditional Islamic method
Compass Arcs
Classical Euclidean approach
1
Draw a circle with radius R centered at point O. This is the bounding circle for the entire pattern.
◯ compass
Produces: base circle — defines the scale of the entire construction
2
Through center O, draw a horizontal and vertical diameter. These create four intersection points on the circle.
╱ straightedge
Produces: two perpendicular diameters — the axes of the first square
3
Connect the four intersection points to inscribe a square. Vertices sit at the compass points: top, right, bottom, left.
╱ straightedge
Produces: first inscribed square — foundation for the 8-fold structure
4
Bisect each 90° arc to find four new points at 45°, 135°, 225°, 315°. Connect to form a second square rotated 45° from the first.
◯ compass╱ straightedge
Produces: second rotated square — the two squares create the 8-pointed star
5
Trace the star outline where the two squares overlap. Connect adjacent intersections to form the inner octagon and extend star points to the bounding circle.
╱ straightedge✎ pen
Produces: the 8-pointed star rosette — trace and ink the final pattern
5 steps · compass + straightedge · ~25 min
